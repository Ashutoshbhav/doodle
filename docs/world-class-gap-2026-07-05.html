DOODLE vs the best clothing websites in the world

Gap analysis and build plan · 5 July 2026 · 10-agent research pass: 5 benchmark researchers (SKIMS, Jacquemus, Aime Leon Dore, Uniqlo, The Souled Store, Snitch, Mini Rodini, Stoney Clover Lane, Hopscotch, FirstCry, plus Baymard Institute and WebAIM research) and 5 auditors over the live site and both repos.

Verdict: 4.2 / 10 overall. The engineering discipline is above the bar for this stage (security, consent, structured data, motion system). What holds the site back is not code, it is proof: there is no real photograph anywhere, the founders are bracketed placeholders, three testimonials are invented, and every date on the live site is in the past. A visitor in July 2026 reading "First drop May 2026" next to "[Founder One]" concludes the company is dead. The good news: roughly half of the severity-5 findings are one-file content edits.

What the best sites taught us (the bar, compressed)

  • Photography is the product. On SKIMS and Jacquemus all colour lives in the images; UI is monochrome and tiny. DOODLE's inversion (colourful illustrated UI, no photos) is exactly backwards for fashion.
  • Commit to one density model. Editorial-tight (SKIMS, 6 sections) or merch-dense (Souled Store, 14,600px). A pre-commerce brand cannot credibly fill a dense page, so run tight: 6-8 folds, one job each.
  • Named drops as franchises. The Souled Store's engine is banner-templated named drops; Mini Rodini does it with owned characters. DOODLE's five packs are five drops waiting for a calendar.
  • Stoney Clover Lane is the proven patch-commerce playbook: cheap repeatable units, a live preview with an honest disclaimer, save/share, and public sellouts. The ₹100 silicone singles are DOODLE's version of their letter patches.
  • Kidswear trust is component-level: height-in-cm sizes with age equivalents next to the buttons, wash-tested claims with numbers, small-parts safety answered before it is asked. 82% of apparel sites under-inform on sizing, so doing this well is cheap differentiation.
  • India checkout is its own standard: pincode-first autofill, UPI-first ordering, visible COD with a nudge to prepaid (5% discount is the observed norm), 15-day exchange as the category floor.
  • Performance is a winnable moat, not table stakes: verified field data shows The Souled Store fails all three Core Web Vitals and Myntra/FirstCry fail two. A lean Next.js site that passes all three beats every reference competitor on measurable UX.
  • The trust voice for an unknown brand is The Whole Truth's: expose one uncomfortable truth (what a ₹999 tee costs to make, or the no-licensed-characters stance as a promise), publish proof, write like a person.

The plan

Phase 0: credibility triage today, ~1 day of edits

Almost entirely one file (src/content/home.ts). Kill everything that makes the site look dead or dishonest.

  • Fix or remove every stale date; switch the drop banner to undated scarcity ("First drop · 200 tees · Bangalore").
  • Unrender EarlyVoices (invented testimonials) and FindUsOffline (past pop-ups) until real content exists.
  • Founders section: real five names (Ronit, Lakshay, Ash, Rohit, Aditya) with one true line each; photos when available.
  • Delete the fake ₹1499 strikethrough; fix "200+" to the derived real patch count; reconcile "6 colours" vs 2 swatches; explain or drop "₹370".
  • Rewrite the two Promise pillars to drop Avengers/Pokemon/Iron Man trademarks (generic "superhero phase" language keeps the insight).
  • Fix the stockist placeholder email, dead footer links, dead anchors; remove TikTok.
  • Waitlist-mode CTAs: nav "Shop the drop" becomes "Join the first drop"; BuildYourTee "Add this kit" becomes "Reserve this kit" scrolling to the waitlist form.

Phase 1: mobile and trust basics this week, code, ₹0

  • Mobile hamburger menu (full-screen sheet: nav links, waitlist CTA, WhatsApp, socials).
  • /faq page (wash durability, velcro feel, small-parts safety, sizing, drop logistics, returns) with FAQPage JSON-LD; /size-guide with the real cm table from the production spec sheet.
  • wa.me click-to-chat in footer, contact page, and mobile menu.
  • Branded 404 page (the patch characters exist for exactly this), 1200x630 OG image under 300KB plus a proper favicon set, canonical fix (remove the root-layout canonical), notFound() for unknown /shop handles, noindex /shop while commerce is off.
  • Touch-target pass: 44px on cart steppers, swatches, remove buttons.
  • Waitlist referral: share link on the success state, referral column in the schema, first-drop allocation ranked by referrals. The highest-leverage free growth loop available.
  • Provision free Upstash so the production rate limiter stops being a no-op.

Phase 2: the funnel gate before the production flip

These block real money. None of them are optional for go-live.

  • Shipping-method step in checkout (fetch options, auto-select free, addShippingMethod, show real rate and ETA).
  • Razorpay HMAC signature verification before cart.complete; webhook completes abandoned-but-paid carts.
  • Server-side overselling guard at add-to-cart and completion.
  • Render the size guide / fabric / care / trust blocks on the live PDP (component already exists); age hints on size buttons; image gallery from product.images with the PatchScrubber as slide one, using real patch PNGs instead of placeholder SVGs.
  • Mini-cart drawer on add-to-cart with a patch cross-sell strip, plus in-cart "add patches" module. Attach rate is the business model.
  • COD pincode serviceability via Shiprocket (autofill city/state, ETA, hide COD where unserviceable). Needs the wallet funded.
  • Fix /account/claim (or point at login); wire the existing shipped/delivered email templates to fulfillment events; back-in-stock capture into the waitlist table.
  • Sticky mobile add-to-cart bar; gift note field at checkout.
  • Legal placeholders filled (entity, address, grievance officer) and the draft banner removed. Hard legal gate under the E-Commerce Rules.

Phase 3: the photography sprint needs Ash, biggest single lever

  • One phone-camera shoot: 2-3 founder/friend kids in the real prototype tees. Fifteen usable shots: hero on-model, hands-on swap sequence, macro of the velcro panel and a silicone patch edge.
  • Cut a 5-8 second muted loop from the existing clip.mp4 (or reshoot): one patch pressed on, peeled off. Place it in the hero card. This is the Crocs Jibbitz move and the highest-converting asset the category has.
  • Revive TheRealThing with real photos (the copy is already written in src/content/home.ts).
  • Re-render or reshoot the six tee colourways from one template so switching colours does not change the garment's shape.
  • Real Instagram with 10+ posts; link it; add a static "From our Instagram" strip.

Phase 4: after launch post-flip, sequenced by revenue

  • Homepage restructure to the tight model: merge PatchMath into PatchWall, fold PacksShowcase to a 3-up teaser, put the on-model story where the stats were.
  • Named-drop calendar: each patch theme becomes a dated drop with its own banner lockup; waitlist gets 24h early access. Plan around late Sep to Diwali, Children's Day (14 Nov), December clearance, June back-to-school.
  • Reviews: request email 7 days post-delivery, store in Neon, render on PDP with AggregateRating JSON-LD. Collect consented quotes with photos at pop-ups meanwhile.
  • Performance moat: strip "use client" from static sections, cache the region lookup, ISR the PLP/PDP, subset fonts to 2 families, then verify all three Core Web Vitals pass (every reference competitor fails at least one).
  • Retention set completion: back-in-stock / next-drop notify first (the waitlist is already the audience), then win-back. Self-serve returns from the order page. Public /track page.
  • Trust voice page: publish what a ₹999 tee costs to make and the no-licensed-characters promise, in first person.
